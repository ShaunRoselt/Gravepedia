# Gravepedia

Gravepedia is a free, community-powered memorial platform that helps people locate graves, preserve photos and inscriptions, and connect family histories. Whether you're tracing ancestry, documenting a local cemetery, or leaving a tribute, Gravepedia makes it simple to find, record, and protect these important pieces of our past.

## What we offer

- Search and discover: Find graves and memorial pages by name, cemetery, or location.
- Photo uploads: Add photographs of headstones and memorials, with optional GPS tagging.
- Transcriptions & records: Transcribe inscriptions to preserve fading text and make records searchable.
- Interactive maps: Explore burial locations visually and navigate to the places that matter.
- Community contributions: Volunteers and family members build and curate memorial pages together.
- Respect-first approach: Content is posted with care and guided by privacy standards.

## How it works

1. **Search** — Start with a name, cemetery, or place to find matching memorials and records.
2. **Explore** — Open memorial pages to view photos, dates, biographical notes, and location details.
3. **Contribute** — Add a photo, upload a transcription, or create a memorial page to share memories and facts. Contributions are credited and can be refined by the community.

## Who it's for

- Families preserving memories and family trees.
- Genealogists and researchers seeking primary burial evidence.
- Local historians and community volunteers documenting cemeteries.
- Anyone who wants to honor and remember people who have passed.

## Our values

- **Preserve:** Protect fragile information before it fades.
- **Respect:** Treat memorials with sensitivity and honor living relatives' privacy.
- **Community:** Collaboration and verification keep records accurate and meaningful.
- **Accessibility:** Make memorial information easy to find and use from any device.

## Get involved

- Explore memorials and start a search.
- Add photos and transcriptions for the places you visit.
- Join local projects to document cemeteries in your area.

Thank you for helping keep stories alive.
